About

I am a researcher trained in social and lifespan psychology with deep expertise in aging, prosocial behavior, and social connectedness. My work bridges the gap between behavioral science and technology — I design user studies, run daily diary experiments, and lead cross-functional teams of engineers, UX designers, and behavioral scientists to build products grounded in human insight.

My recent focus is on AI × Aging × Social Connection: understanding how older adults interact with AI systems, what ethical guardrails matter, and how we can design inclusive digital products for an aging world. This work has been recognized with the Healthy Longevity Catalyst Award (2024), where our interdisciplinary team developed an AI-powered storytelling platform that went from research concept to functional prototype.

I am seeking opportunities to continue translating research into impact — whether as a postdoctoral researcher at a top institution or an industry research scientist / UX researcher at companies shaping social technology, human-AI interaction, and responsible AI.

Featured Project

Healthy Longevity Catalyst Award 2024

Tales Weaver: An AI-Enhanced System to Foster Social Connectedness

Bridging behavioral science and AI engineering to build a product that helps older adults stay socially connected through digital storytelling.

Tales Weaver is an age-friendly digital storytelling platform powered by AI speech recognition and image recognition. It enables older adults to capture personal narratives and share them across generations, reducing social isolation and fostering mutual understanding.

This project exemplifies research-to-product translation: I served as project manager and Co-Investigator, leading the collaboration across behavioral scientists, engineers, and UX designers. I designed the user study framework — including daily diary protocols — to rigorously evaluate engagement and intervention outcomes.

A Unique Interdisciplinary Collaboration

This project was made possible by the convergence of two world-class research groups:

  • Prof. Helene H. Fung (Psychology, CUHK) — Stanford Ph.D., Chair of Psychology at CUHK, Fellow of APA, APS, and GSA, recipient of the Baltes Award in Behavioral & Social Gerontology. Ranked in the world's top 2% of scientists (Stanford). Her decades of work on socioemotional aging and motivation provided the theoretical backbone for why and how narrative sharing enhances well-being.
  • Prof. Helen Meng (Engineering, CUHK) — MIT-trained, Fellow of IEEE and ISCA, leading authority in multilingual speech and language processing, conversational AI, and human-computer interaction. Her team built the multilingual AI speech and image recognition engine that makes the platform accessible and intelligent.

As the researcher connecting these two domains, I translated psychological theory into product requirements and designed evaluation studies to measure real-world impact — the kind of work that matters whether you are shipping a feature at a tech company or publishing in a top journal.

Why This Matters for Industry

Social Platforms & Community

Understanding prosocial behavior, social perception, and cross-cultural user differences is critical for platforms like Meta, ByteDance, and others building products for billions. My research provides evidence-based insight into what drives connection and engagement across age groups and cultures.

Responsible AI & Trust

As AI becomes embedded in everyday products, companies need researchers who understand the human side: How do users perceive AI? What ethical considerations matter for vulnerable populations? My work on ageism and AI adoption directly addresses these questions.

User Research at Scale

I bring rigorous mixed-method skills — experiments, surveys, diary studies, social media analytics, and focus groups — to generate deep user insights that go beyond surface-level metrics. I am trained in both quantitative and qualitative methods at the highest academic standard.
